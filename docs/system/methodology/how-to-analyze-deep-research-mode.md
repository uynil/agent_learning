# How to Analyze Deep Research Mode

## Goal

Understand how a project turns a broad question into a multi-round investigation that can search, read, synthesize, revise, and stop at the right time.

## Checklist

1. Identify the broad question input
2. Trace whether the question is rewritten or decomposed
3. Map the search-read-synthesize loop
4. Check how evidence is accumulated across rounds
5. Check whether critique, reflection, or reviewer stages exist
6. Check how each round is summarized for the next round
7. Check the stop conditions
8. Check whether the process is resumable after interruption

## Questions to Ask

- How is the original question narrowed into actionable research questions?
- Are subquestions generated explicitly or implicitly?
- How are sources and evidence tracked across rounds?
- How are contradictions handled?
- When does the loop decide to stop or pivot?
- Can another session resume without replaying the full history?

## Output Pattern

- Research Loop Overview
- Question Rewrite Strategy
- Decomposition Strategy
- Evidence Accumulation
- Reflection and Revision
- Stop Conditions
- Resumability and Context Handoff
- Reusable Patterns
